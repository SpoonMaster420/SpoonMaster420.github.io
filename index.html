Boolean Logic

The AND gate takes two input values and produces and output of 1 if both inputs are 1. This may be written as:
  • A^B
  • A.B
  • AB
The OR gate takes two input values and produces an output of 1 if either one of the inputs are 1. This may be written as:
  • AvB
  • A+B
The NOT gate takes a single input value and flips it to its logical opposite. This may be written as:
  • ¬A
  • Ā
  • |A
The XOR gate takes two input values and produces an output of 1 if either but not both inputs are 1. This is the 'exclusive OR' and is also sometimes called EOR. This may be written as:
  • AvB
